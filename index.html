How Different Wines Are Being Made?

Winemaking is the production of wine, starting with the selection of grapes, fermentation into alcohol and the bottling of the finished liquid. The main wine categories are White and Red Wine. White wine is primarily made with white grapes, and the skins are separated from the juice before the fermentation process while Red Wine is made with darker red or black grapes, and the skins remain on the grapes during the fermentation process. Dry wines contain residual sugar with less than 1% and wines containing residual sugar with more than 10% are considered as sweet wines (Fadamitan, 2022).

What Is In A Glass Of Wine?

In general, wine contains 70% of water, 20% of ethanol and 10% of other ingredients such as sugar, acids and phenols. Acidity in wines can range from extremely sour at around 2.5 pH (high acidity) to very flat at around 4.5 pH (low acidity). According to Puckette (2022), white wines tend to have higher acidity than red wines. This is due to the ripeness of the grapes. Cooler climates produce highly acidic wines with very little naturally occurring sugars. On the other hand, warmer climates often produce wines with lower acidity. The high temperatures cause grapes to ripen rapidly, reducing the naturally occurring acids and increasing the sweetness of grapes and resulting wine (McKenzie, 2020).

The average glass of wine contains about 11% to 13% alcohol. However, different wine varieties contain a different amount of alcohol. The visualisation below displays the range of alcohol content in terms of Alcohol By Volume (ABV). Drinkers will notice that the back of their mouth or throat will feel warm due to the alcohol consumed. A wine with a greater ABV would taste bolder and warmer, almost as if the palate were being slightly burned. Top Varieties of Wine in 2017

Pinot Noir is the world's most popular light-bodied red wine in 2017. Itâ€™s loved for its red fruit, flower, and spice aromas that are accentuated by a long, smooth finish. Pinot Noir pairs particularly well with duck, chicken, pork, and mushrooms. Besides that, it is considered as the best red wine for beginners. On the other hand, Chardonnay is the most popular white wine worldwide in 2017. Chardonnay is made in a wide range of styles from lean, sparkling Blanc de Blancs to rich, creamy white wines aged in oak. Chardonnay is usually paired with meaty fish (halibut, cod) and shellfish (lobster, shrimp, crab, scallops). It is also now the most widely planted white grape variety, globally (Puckette, 2022).


Prices of The Top 10 Wine Varieties based on Reviews

Based on the boxplot visualisation below, the highest median price of red wine is Pinot Noir and Chardonnay for white wine.
